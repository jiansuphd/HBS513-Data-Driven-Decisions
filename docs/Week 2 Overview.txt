    <hr style="border-top: 3px solid orange;" />
    <h3><span style="color: #58595b;"><strong><img id="12823656" role="presentation" src="https://utk.instructure.com/courses/242597/files/28448522/preview" alt="" width="60" height="60" data-api-endpoint="https://utk.instructure.com/api/v1/courses/242597/files/28448522" data-api-returntype="File" /> Overview</strong></span></h3>
    <p>Welcome back to Stata! Week 2 marks our first applied &ldquo;lab&rdquo; of HBS 513. Rather than re-learning everything from 512, our focus is on refreshing the core coding skills you need to succeed in this course&rsquo;s causal inference toolkit. Think of this as knocking the rust off: we&rsquo;ll load data, generate new variables, run summaries and regressions, and produce visuals &mdash;you know, some of the basic commands you&rsquo;ll rely on every week going forward.</p>
    <p>This module is fully hands-on. You&rsquo;ll watch a guided walkthrough and replicate the workflow step-by-step in your own Stata session. If you follow along carefully, you&rsquo;ll end the week with a clean dataset, working code, and (hopefully) the confidence heading into the heavier modules we have coming.</p>
    <p><strong>A NOTE ON STATA</strong><em>: Yes, you have to use Stata.</em> Why? Because from here on out, Excel just can&rsquo;t keep up. Stata gives us replicability, precise control over data cleaning and transformation, and the ability to implement real causal models used in policy research and professional analysis. Once you re-establish the basics, Stata becomes faster, clearer, and way more scalable than any spreadsheet.</p>

        <li>Run simple regression models using regress</li>
        <li>Create basic data visualizations (e.g., histograms, scatterplots)</li>
