    <h2><span style="color: #4b4b4b;"><strong>Interrupted Time Series</strong></span></h2>
    <hr style="border-top: 3px solid orange;" />
    <h3><span style="color: #58595b;"> <strong><img role="presentation" src="https://utk.instructure.com/courses/242597/files/28448522/preview" alt="Overview icon" width="60" height="60" data-api-endpoint="https://utk.instructure.com/api/v1/courses/242597/files/28448522" data-api-returntype="File" /> Overview</strong> </span></h3>
    <p>Week 7&nbsp;introduces Interrupted Time Series (ITS), a design used to estimate the impact of a policy or event when something meaningful happens at a specific moment in time. Instead of comparing different units to one another, ITS compares a unit to <em>itself</em> over time, using the intervention date as a cutoff.</p>
    <p>When the pre-intervention trend is stable, and the policy is sharp and well-timed, ITS allows us to see whether the outcome changes right when it should. This makes ITS especially valuable for evaluating real-world policies&mdash;laws, mandates, bans, program launches&mdash;that roll out on a known date. In this module, we&rsquo;ll walk through the logic of the design, estimate effects using simulated data, and learn what makes ITS results credible and compelling for public sector decision-making.</p>
    <hr style="border-top: 3px solid orange;" />
    <h3><span style="color: #58595b;"> <strong><img role="presentation" src="https://utk.instructure.com/courses/242597/files/28448522/preview" alt="Learning Objectives icon" width="60" height="60" data-api-endpoint="https://utk.instructure.com/api/v1/courses/242597/files/28448522" data-api-returntype="File" /> Learning Objectives</strong> </span></h3>
    <p>By the end of this module, you will be able to:</p>
    <ul>
        <li>Explain how ITS identifies causal effects using a clear intervention date.</li>
        <li>Describe the essential features that make ITS a valid and interpretable design.</li>
        <li>Interpret ITS results from both visual plots and regression models.</li>
        <li>Determine when ITS is an appropriate method&mdash;and when it is not&mdash;for answering policy questions.</li>
        <li>Recognize the strengths and limitations of ITS in applied public administration and policy analysis.</li>
    </ul>
