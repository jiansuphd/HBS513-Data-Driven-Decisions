Regression Discontinuity

Overview

Week 5 introduces Regression Discontinuity (RD), a practical tool for evaluating policy impact when programs use a clear cutoff—like age, income, or score—to determine who gets treated. Rather than needing a randomized experiment, RD lets us compare people just above and below the threshold to see if the treatment made a difference. We’ll focus on the logic behind the design, how to estimate effects using real data, and what makes RD results trustworthy. For public administrators, this is a powerful way to learn what works—using the policies and thresholds already built into government programs.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

  • Explain how regression discontinuity designs identify causal effects using real-world policy cutoffs.
  • Describe the key assumptions that make RD designs valid and interpretable.
  • Interpret RD results from both visual plots and regression models.
  • Evaluate when RD is an appropriate method for answering policy questions.
  • Recognize the strengths and limitations of RD for public sector decision-making.
